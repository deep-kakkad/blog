Back-story and how it was built

So, Sahil Lavingia - founder of Gumroad and 2nd employee at Pinterest is like an ideal to me. His tweets (and even just a handful of them) and other writeups, I believe has taught me more than books.

Yesterday, he tweeted something in the lines of - 'Do something even before it has a name'. I don't know how it interconnected and inspired me to read all of his tweets. This sounds crazy, but I really wish to one day, read all of his 26.5k tweets.

This led me to the idea of collecting some of his best tweets in one place.

Caution - I'm sleepy after working on this for like 7 hours straight (or maybe more, I may have lost the count haha), after work. This may have some typos, but I wanted to get my lessons out here as raw as possible :)

So, coming back.

Starts a quick side-project brainstorming around midnight.

First go-to was doing a PH like upvote-y thing. I could've done this using an app called Pory, but I did a very similar thing last week and wanted to get out that comfort zone of using the same tool, plus I wanted something better looking.

Went on to bubble to build this...

https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/NE3w5TaPraiMATJtdMHHPJOw2Zcil1TPh9MVwP7Rv13s-lx2DqUA9P8yddLyrGxQtT2eEMdjv-eoehem26OP-Qs9Kd4bCf1vZmnPpRWM-AVvlOSTgnlHGCjIQ0w5ZtXf0HRx3cGI

After spending what felt like an hour, I had this working prototype but not something I'd be proud to share with Sahil. Plus, this also seemed to be consuming more time than I expected.

For the sake of simplicity, I moved to Notion, where after again going through tons of templates I settled for this simple thing.

https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/qGxOejc4Z8E5zePemKFErysZAqumnxUf277_mgbWqf_-Bt9fexlTrAiLQRXHryi-TM7dDTKtDTh5xW9ClqrKNReJ2U3UAgBRPztuN2QcvT-eQVx4RcDndyPaGuvUpPBvnn_Q27An

A few tweets in, and I dropped on this too. Looked too simple and boring.

While building on both of these tools, I felt I wanted something better looking. Hence, the next tool - Webflow.

Now, I've used Webflow CMS a bit and tried my hands on designing on Webflow, but even after being good at HTML, CSS, Javascript - couldn't get a hang on Webflow designing concepts.

Went to Flowbase to check if I could use any of their Webflow templates to make it easier for me. Tried a few templates, but here are the problems I felt with them -

  1. It was hard to manipulate the templates to make them work my way (as a newbie)
  2. I felt I wasn't learning anything

Nothing better than starting from scratch, I thought. And I feel that was the best decision.

Started a Webflow project from a blank screen. Played around with rich text elements.

https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-j9sCxiaIsF6gwZNcPLdIWU4GFf8za46WiHFSljJI_0CnvM5egekiFt8NI7O048q2T2556Id5wHSkWy7cj_z2w2shZ5pKfFDyha5S2QCUosKVg9uNA1_L_DRDnO_gugTKHAVtbeq
